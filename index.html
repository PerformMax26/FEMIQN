01

Pre-Launch Checklist

Before the team sets up a single campaign, these non-negotiables must be confirmed. Do not spend a dollar on paid media until every item below is ticked off.

  • Meta Pixel installed and firing correctly on all key pages — View Content, Add to Cart, Initiate Checkout, Purchase
  • Google Tag Manager set up with GA4 and Google Ads conversion tracking live
  • Hero product SKUs finalised — 10 to 15 products maximum. Do not run ads to the full catalogue
  • Product pages updated with benefit-led copy before driving paid traffic to them
  • Free shipping offer made clearly visible on site — currently buried
  • 15% off first order offer confirmed as the lead acquisition hook for all cold traffic

Google Ads — Campaign Structure

Google Ads

Campaign 1 — Branded Search

Type: Search

Protect the brand name. Even with a small budget this is essential — competitors may be bidding on the brand name, and the 45+ audience frequently searches for a brand they have seen on TVSN or social before purchasing.

Keywords
femiqn femiqn.com femiqn fashion femiqn australia
Settings
  • Match types: Exact and Phrase
  • Bidding: Maximise Clicks initially, then Target CPA once data builds
~$3–5/day

Campaign 2 — Non-Brand Search (Core Product Intent)

Type: Search

Target women actively searching for what FEMIQN sells. These are high-intent buyers at the bottom of the funnel.

Priority keyword themes
women's pull-on pants australia comfortable pants women over 40 women's elastic waist pants flattering jumpsuits for women australia women's clothing australia plus size women's fashion 40s 50s australia body positive women's clothing ponte pants women australia
Negative keywords — add from day one
men's kids wholesale cheap fast fashion teen juniors
Settings
  • Match types: Phrase and Broad Match — monitored closely
  • Bidding: Maximise Conversions once 30+ conversions tracked, else Maximise Clicks
~$20–30/day

Campaign 3 — Google Shopping

Type: Performance Max or Standard Shopping

Hero products only — do not push the full catalogue to Shopping. Confirm Shopify Google channel or Google Merchant Centre product feed is live and approved before launching.

Feed requirements
  • Product titles must include fit descriptors, size range and key benefit
  • If launching Performance Max, exclude branded search terms via campaign settings to avoid cannibalising Campaign 1
Asset group creative (for PMax)
  • Headlines: align to messaging framework — trust, fit, comfort
  • Images: hero product shots and lifestyle imagery of women 45+
  • Videos: add 15–30 sec clips if available
~$20–25/day

Campaign 4 — Display Remarketing

Type: Display
Audiences
  • Website visitors — last 30 days
  • Add to Cart — last 14 days
  • Exclude purchasers from both
Creative

Banner ads with hero product imagery and trust messaging — e.g. "Still thinking about it? Free shipping + 15% off your first order"

~$5–10/day
Note: YouTube can be added as a future layer once creative video assets are available.

08

Key Guardrails for the Team

These rules are non-negotiable throughout the campaign. Deviating from them risks wasted spend, brand damage or poor conversion on a tight budget.

Do not send paid traffic to category pages. Send to specific hero product pages with strong, benefit-led copy.

Do not run ads to any product that does not have an updated, benefit-led product description live on the page.

Do not run discounting ads without client approval. The brand is positioned as accessible premium — not a sale brand.

Do not launch Shopping without first confirming the product feed is approved in Google Merchant Centre and product titles are fully optimised.

Keep the 15% first-order offer consistent across all Meta and Google landing pages. Message match is critical for the 45+ buyer who is deliberate and selective.
